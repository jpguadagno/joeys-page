the fit out
Instructor Erin Besler, TA Joshua Nelson and Alex Abugov (UCLA 2018)

In a law firm, hierarchy is vital. Fitting out such an office therefore requires commitment to order and structure.

The first three plans make such commitments: associates are placed into bullpens overseen by a partner. Further grouping these into repeatable cellblocks, a grid is produced, which in turn provides order to other program elements. When faced with the challenge of an irregular plan, priority is given to the grid. At the expense of clipped cellblocks, the order of the fit-out is upheld.

The fourth plan serves as a rebuke of hierarchy. This level is shared by the three firms for meetings of firms and with clients. An open plan with minimal partitions and a circular grid provides relief from the rigid order of the private floors, and conference tables are organized with little regard for any system.

Project in collaboration with Michael Lee. Diagrams, plans and first model are my own contribution. Base plan provided by Nicole Fan and Olivia Kope.
